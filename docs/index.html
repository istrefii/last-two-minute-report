NBA Last Two Minute Report

By Russell Goldenberg

Last updated Feb. 14, 2017

The NBA has been reviewing the officiating of the last two minutes of close games as part of a transparency initiative. This project is an on-going effort to enhance this transparency by making the data more accessible.

Since March 2015, the NBA has reviewed 12,857 plays from 827 games. In those 2,529 minutes of action, the officials have missed or incorrectly called 1,350 plays, or about 10.5% of all calls reviewed. This amounts to 1.63 wrong decisions in the final minutes of each close game.

Player bias

Is star treatment a thing in the last two minutes? A call is deemed in favor of a player if it was missed call on them (committing player + INC) or a wrong call against their opponent (disadvantaged player + IC). The opposite logic applies calculate a call that opposes a player.
